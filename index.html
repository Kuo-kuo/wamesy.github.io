    • Cyberbulls!

    When we upload cows to the internet, obviously, we get cybercows. If you did not know this, then you should probably go back to elementary school.

    Less obvious is the fact that there are male and female cows. If you were not aware of this, then I recommend taking Cowology 101.

    In late 2017, I visited the German Cow Station (GCS) and met a class 3-Heimholtz-ψ cow-monk, who gave me the following information after I bribed her with two (2) kilograms of gluten-free marshmallows. A recent expedition to Canada uncovered some long-forgotten biology lore, detailing how male cows (the ones with testicles (don't touch the testicles)) are called "bulls." If you have a bull and need to give it a name, I recommend "Moobert."

    Consequently, we can see that a bull that has been uploaded to the internet would be called a "cyberbull." Contrary to popular belief, cyberbulls are not dangerous. However, cyberbulls often attempt to send hate mail to cyberhogs. If you stumble upon a cyberbull that is attempting to do so, it is best to back away slowly while chanting the Litany of Cybercreature Acceptance in a low, monotone voice. Honestly nobody wants to deal with a cyberbull if it's sending hate mail to cyberhogs, and it's a huge waste of the United States Postal Service's time.
